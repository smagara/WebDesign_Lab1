Tim Berners-Lee

Inventor of the Internet


Tim Berners-Lee speaking at the launch of the World Wide Web Foundation.

Photo by Scott Henrichsen, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons
  • 1955 Born in London, England
  • 1976 BA Physics, Oxford
  • 1980 Initial Hypertext project at CERN
  • 1980 Works on network RPCs at Image Computer Systems, Ltd
  • 1984 Returns to CERN and links Hypertext with Network concepts
  • 1990 Proposes the World Wide Web as an amalgam of Internet routing, Hypertext, and multifont text. Hosts his proposal on the first Website.
  • 1991 Posts on Usenet a public invitation to the WorldWideWeb Project
  • 1994 Founds WC3 Consortium at MIT for royalty-free standards
  • 2009 Launches World Wide Web Foundation to "advance the Web to empower humanity"
  • 2012 Is honored at the Summer Olympics as the inventor of the Internet
